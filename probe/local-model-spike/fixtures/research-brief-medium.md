# Research Brief — Remote Work and Productivity

This brief reviews recent findings on remote work and team productivity. It is intended as an internal summary, not a peer-reviewed publication.

## Background

Remote work expanded sharply after 2020. Many teams now operate in a hybrid model. The evidence on productivity is mixed and often contradictory.

## Key Findings

One study reported a 13% productivity gain among remote call-center staff. A different survey found no significant change in output for knowledge workers. Self-reported focus improved in most samples, but collaboration scores declined.

The 13% productivity gain figure is frequently cited, though its sample was narrow.

## Limitations

Most studies rely on self-reported data, which is unreliable. Sample sizes vary widely. The 13% productivity gain figure comes from a single industry and may not generalize.

## Methodology

We reviewed twelve studies published between 2021 and 2025. Studies were included if they measured output directly. The review was completed on May 9, 2025.

## Recommendations

Teams should pilot hybrid schedules before committing. Managers should measure outcomes, not hours. Further internal data collection is recommended before drawing firm conclusions.

## Caveats

These conclusions are preliminary. The research base is still evolving. This caveats section is accurate and complete and requires no edits.
