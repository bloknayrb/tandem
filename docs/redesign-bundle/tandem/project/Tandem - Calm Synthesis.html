Tandem · calm synthesis · 2026-05-13

One direction.
The best move from each.

Pulling the strongest atomic move from each of the five explorations. The chrome goes borderless (A), the editor lifts as a paper sheet (B), the rail stays docked but its cards lose borders, gain kind tints, and align vertically to their anchors with a thin tinted leader (C+E), and frost is reserved for things that genuinely float — mini-toolbar, palette, popovers (D).

The result has one fewer surface, no hairlines anywhere, and far less visual noise — but the structure of the app is unchanged. Existing affordances are all still there; they just stop fighting for attention.

AOne Paper

Borderless titlebar, tabs, status

All chrome rows sit on the warm canvas. Status bar is plain mono labels — no top border.

BSheet on Desk

Editor as a paper sheet

Centered sheet at 0.998 lightness on a 0.945 canvas. Soft shadow only; no border. The doc reads as the primary object.

CMargin Notes

Anchor-aligned cards + leader

Cards in the docked rail position vertically to their anchor in the editor. A thin tinted leader connects card to anchor on hover or selection.

DFrosted Overlays

Frost only for transient floats

Mini-toolbar, command palette, popovers — frost glass. Docked surfaces stay opaque so the eye doesn't fight the document.

ETinted Shells

Borderless rail cards, kind tint

Annotation cards lose their 1px border entirely. A soft per-kind tint (yellow/violet/red/coral) plus a 3px tinted left rail carries the type.
