Sub-PR 1.11 · Option 2 · card-shaped surface (scratch — not committed)

Everything in one surface — as a card, not a pill

Option 2 holds every formatting control plus the annotate action. A pill's geometry fights that density. A card (radius --tandem-r-4, --tandem-shadow-2) lets the content group into a transform zone and an annotate zone with a real divider between them. Three card shapes below. Note: this surface stops sharing the .tandem-floating-pill recipe — a deliberate divergence, since the content differs.

Interactive — heading caret, Annotate, swatches, segment. Switch themes to check contrast.

Theme
Grouping follows Tandem's data model, not generic toolbar habit. The honest split is Format (operations that change the document text — inline marks + block type) vs Annotate (operations that create entries in Y.Map('annotations') — and highlight is an annotation type, not a text mark). So highlight belongs with Note/Comment in the annotate zone, not next to Bold/Italic. Within Format, inline marks (B/I/S/code/link) and block type (heading/lists/quote/code-block/rule) are the two natural sub-rows.

Shape A — sectioned card

Recommended

Format zone on top (inline-marks row, then block-type row), a full-width divider, then the Annotate zone (highlight row + Note/Comment). The divider maps exactly to the data-model split: above changes the text, below creates annotations. Heading caret sits at top level of the block row — no nested-popover problem.

Recommendation

Shape A — sectioned card. Annotate footer reveals the composer inline (default), so the card stays short until you're actually writing.

  • Why a card beats the pill here: the internal divider makes the transform/annotate split legible, the moderate radius reads as "a panel of tools" instead of "a row of quick actions," and rows can group naturally without the pill forcing one tall horizontal run.
  • A over B: the grid is handsome but wider and more icon-dependent; A's two grouped rows are tighter and the dividers already imply grouping without needing labels.
  • A over C: C is great if commenting dominates, but it's always tall and pushes formatting into a side role. A keeps formatting first-class and only grows to compose height when you click Comment/Note.
  • Heading caret stays top-level in all three — this avoids the nested-popover wrinkle that the pill's chevron-expand designs had.
Still Option 2, so the caveats hold: undo / redo / authorship are not selection-scoped and move to the title bar; this is a 2–3 PR sequence (build card → relocate controls → delete FormattingBar → retire its testids → re-point tutorial anchors → Conflict #2/#5 override). Leaving the pill recipe is an added, intentional divergence.
